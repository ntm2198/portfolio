Teaching Philosophy

Computer science as inquiry, craft, and expression.

My classroom starts with questions students can touch: How does a rule become a game? How can a sensor change a story? What makes a simulation feel fair, surprising, or alive? From there, code becomes a medium for testing ideas and making thinking visible.

I blend liberal arts discussion, collaborative critique, and iterative prototyping so that rigorous technical work feels accessible to students with different histories, identities, and entry points into STEM.
