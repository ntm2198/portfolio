Road Ready Rally

Game-Based Skill Building for students with ADHD

A physical board game designed to be used in-class to scaffold execuitive functioning skills for middle school students with ADHD.

Project Story

We asked: how can board games be used to help middle schoolers with ADHD build execuitive functioning skills within a traditional learning environment.

What this was

Road Ready Rally is an inclusive, tangible educational board game engineered to bolster executive functioning and materials organization skills for neurodiverse middle school students. Grounded in Zimmerman’s Self-Regulated Learning Model and Flow Theory, the gameplay naturally balances sensory regulation with targeted cognitive challenges to maintain an optimal instructional state. Crucially, the platform features a highly adaptive architecture, empowering K-12 STEM educators to seamlessly substitute customized academic review decks tailored directly to their active curriculum standards. Through this integration of behavioral science and versatile design, the game transforms routine classroom assessment into a dynamic, low-pressure cooperative learning experience.

Learning goals

  • Executive Functioning & Planning: Students will develop spatial planning, resource management, and predictive thinking skills by navigating multi-step game sequences and adapting strategy to shifting board dynamics.
  • Sensory & Behavioral Self-Regulation: Students will practice emotional regulation and focus-maintenance under timed conditions by leveraging physical, tactile interaction mechanics (such as interactive "fidget" components) designed to channel hyperactive energy into deliberate gameplay tasks.
  • Collaborative Communication: Students will practice pro-social peer interactions, collaborative problem-solving, and negotiation skills by working in cooperative team structures to overcome interactive learning challenges.

Learning theory

“In the context of playing games, the learning content is emergent rather than explicit. These games represent a challenge to solve, for which players must implement their cognitive abilities and knowledge that belongs to the curricular content.” (Vita-Barrull et al., 2024)
